# EHR Requirements

This document sets out the requirements for a new EHR. It will have the principles of openness and interoperability at its core. The EHR will ensure it meets the needs of all its users and maintain the patient at the centre as a core priority. It will encourage patient engagement and involvement in their healthcare data. It will be the key way to integrate safely the rapid advancements in areas such as AI, genomics and health data to shift from reactive healthcare to a system that utilises the principles of healthcare that is personalised, predictive, preventative and participatory.

Health systems represent a key piece of national security infrastructure. They need to be secure, resistant to attacks, and have the ability for vulnerabilities once identified to be rapidly removed. Availability, uptime and reliability is key.

When considering the requirements of an EHR it is essential to consider the needs of the people that will be using it.

## Key Stakeholders

- Clinician
- Patient
- Healthcare trust/provider
- Developers

## Clinician Needs

The clinician has many requirements of an EHR. The key ones can be summarised as:

- Promote and optimise continuity of care
- Produce an accurate, truly representative summary of a clinical encounter
- Optimise the ability of the clinician to deliver safe, high quality clinical care

Other requirements include but are not limited to:

- Enable effective communication with other members of the healthcare team
- Bidirectional patient communication
- Streamlined requesting of investigations and the co-ordination of results
- Prescription management
- Medicolegal considerations/support

### Promote and Optimise Continuity of Care

The EHR should utilise knowledge graphs to build a representation of a patient journey. This identifies that the relationships between data points are just as important as the data itself. Each clinical consultation, test result, procedure and referral forms a part of the knowledge graph. The interface allows automatic presentation of condition relevant data and this also allows the potential for AI-enabled search functions and monitoring.

The system will recognise and build in the value of the human clinical relationship in care. The system will promote and support the ability to keep continuity where possible and correct.

The patient journey from initial presentation, through investigation and diagnosis will be a shared collaborative workflow with engagement of the patient and clinician. The graph will be built on together. A patient can see relevant and appropriate information about the path and trajectory of this journey and so feels informed of what is happening with their care.

### Produce an Accurate, Truly Representative Summary of a Clinical Encounter

This requires a hybrid approach of both structured templates and free-form data. Condition-specific templates provide an effective way to ensure correct and detailed data collection as well as structured recording.

Medical records represent more than just a set of data points for a condition. They need to represent the clinician's thought process and interpretation of the encounter — explaining how they came to conclusions and why certain decisions were made. There may be prompts, small details and facts that do not directly relate to the presentation but form a vital part of the clinical documentation. This deeper context is important. It facilitates recall in future encounters and can provide valuable context in the evaluation of a presentation, the wider clinical picture and guide future decisions. It should not be overlooked.

### Optimise the Ability of the Clinician to Deliver Safe, High Quality Clinical Care

The EHR is an active tool, central to the consultation. It is not an abstract diary that is filled in at the end of an encounter. It sits centrally with the patient and clinician working with it to facilitate care.

Context-relevant data is surfaced either from pre-existing investigations or encounters. The EHR also provides easy access to both internal and external resources — referral criteria, guidelines, patient information resources.

The EHR will have native AI integration. This will enable information retrieval, analysis and interrogation. The AI outputs need to be complete, correct and provable. The provenance and ‘memory of the why’ needs to be visible and preserved.

## Patient Needs

### Core Functionality

- The information displayed to the patient needs to be appropriate — understandable, not harmful, containing sufficient detail whilst remaining user friendly.
- Integrates with the NHS App.
- Patient involvement from the point of contact. Current triage tools are often working in separate systems/platforms. The triage should become the foundation of the clinical encounter — moving away from being a workload allocation tool to forming a truly valuable and constructive part of the patient journey. This can enable pre-screening, questionnaires, initiation of tests, automated referrals/signposting, and pre-appointment briefing that sets realistic expectations and agendas. It will help ensure the patient comes to the appointment prepared. It should not be overburdensome, however detailed support at this stage can provide a strong foundation for the next steps in the care journey.
- Patients need a way to be an active participant in their healthcare journey — informed, supported, engaged, heard.
- Accessible.
- Trust and transparency — easily accessible information around data sharing, use of health data for research and other use cases.

### Additional Functionality

- Patients can extract and use data about them for other purposes/applications.
- Integration with wearables data.
- Ability to sit within broader health/wellness/lifestyle applications.

---

## Healthcare Providers

- Need to be able to coordinate service requirements, demands, planning, efficiency and governance.
- Audit.
- Data sharing.
- Staff/user access management.
- Avoid downtime and security breaches.
- Ability to add tools and functionality from a thriving, supported market to meet service requirements.

---

## Developers

- Need a usable and structured framework on which to build their applications.
- Strong interoperability.
- Avoidance of clashes between different systems.
- A strong, supported and dynamic market.
